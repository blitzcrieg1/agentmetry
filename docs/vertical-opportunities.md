# Where BLACKBOX Can Be Really Successful

Deep research on verticals, geographies, and buyer profiles where governed,
vault-first automation has the **highest probability of adoption**—beyond
K-beauty e-commerce.

Scoring criteria (each 1–5, max 25):

| Criterion | Why it matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Document load** | Notes, email, meetings = core BLACKBOX input |
| **Repeatability** | Same workflows weekly → skills + triggers win over chat |
| **Approval need** | Outbound/regulated → Gate is a feature not friction |
| **Solo/micro team** | No IT staff; owner is the operator (9% admin time in EU SMEs) |
| **Local-first appeal** | GDPR, client confidentiality, “our files stay ours” |

**Tier S** = 20–25 · **Tier A** = 16–19 · **Tier B** = 12–15 · **Poor** = &lt;12

Sources: NSBA 2025, KfW SME bureaucracy 2025, UK BEIS compliance study, Constant
Contact SMB 2026, project fit analysis.

---

## Tier S — Highest success probability

### 1. Professional services agencies (5–25 people)

**Examples:** marketing, dev shops, design, PR, management consulting.

| Score | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Document | 5 — client calls, briefs, SOWs, retros |
| Repeat | 5 — every client, every week |
| Approval | 4 — client-facing copy |
| Solo/micro | 4 — often 3–15 staff |
| Local-first | 4 — client NDAs |

**BLACKBOX today:** meeting summarize, inbox triage, weekly review, proposal
drafts from vault RAG.

**With drivers:** client email drafts (Gmail), status reports, follow-up cron.

**Pitch:** *“Client work memory + meeting→tasks without a PM hire.”*

**EU/Greece note:** NSBA-class data: “professional, scientific, technical
services” is largest regulated SMB cohort (~37% in US sample); Greece/EU agencies
face VAT, contract, and labor documentation—vault audit trail helps.

---

### 2. B2B import / distribution / wholesale

**Examples:** K-beauty, food import, industrial parts, fashion wholesale.

| Score | 23/25 |

**Why Tier S:** supplier email volume, MOQ/COA docs, outreach campaigns, margin
notes—all document-native. Woo optional; Gmail + search is the killer combo.

**Best workflows:** supplier_outreach, customer_reply (B2B buyers), RFQ responder.

**Geography:** EU cross-border (Greece → EU) = shipping/customs notes in vault.

---

### 3. Solo consultants & fractional executives

**Examples:** strategy, ops, HR, IT consultant.

| Score | 22/25 |

**Why:** NSBA/KfW—**solo entrepreneurs spend ~9% of time on admin** (highest
burden). One person = BLACKBOX operator. Obsidian already popular in this crowd.

**Today fit:** Excellent—no integrations required to start.

**Monetization:** Personal productivity → word of mouth → “install for clients.”

---

### 4. Grant writing & nonprofits (small)

**Examples:** NGOs, cultural orgs, EU fund applicants (Greece: ESPA, Horizon
partners).

| Score | 21/25 |

**Why:** Massive document assembly, strict wording, retention obligations (52%
of SMEs cite documentation burden—KfW). Approval before submission is natural.

**Skills:** summarize sources, draft sections from vault templates, checklist
compliance against call text (human submits).

**Caution:** Don’t auto-submit grants; draft + human review only.

---

### 5. Boutique recruiting & talent agencies

**Examples:** 2–10 recruiter shops.

| Score | 20/25 |

**Why:** Candidate notes, client JDs, outreach email volume, follow-ups—classic
lead_gen + triage. GDPR-sensitive (CVs)—**local vault** is a sales point in EU.

**With drivers:** Gmail sequences with approval; search for company intel.

---

## Tier A — Strong fit with one integration or vertical pack

### 6. Real estate (small agencies, 3–15 agents)

| Score | 19/25 |

Notes on listings, client preferences, viewing logs → summarize + follow-up
drafts. CRM (HubSpot/Pipedrive) driver later. High email volume.

**Greece:** strong small agency market; multilingual (GR/EN) drafts from vault.

---

### 7. Construction / trades subcontractors

| Score | 18/25 |

KfW: **construction SMEs ~8% admin time**. Site notes, quotes, permit
checklists, safety docs—paste to Inbox → triage. Not plan takeoff software.

**EU:** WHS/documentation burden cited heavily in AU/EU regulation studies—
checklist skills from vault SOPs.

---

### 8. Bookkeeping & accounting practices (micro)

| Score | 17/25 |

**Not** replacing Xero/Elorus—**prep layer:** client doc triage, missing-info
chase emails (draft), meeting summarize with client. Accountants outsource
interpretation to owners (UK BEIS)—BLACKBOX helps **before** the ledger.

---

### 9. Insurance & mortgage brokers (small)

| Score | 17/25 |

Comparison notes, client fact-finds, policy summaries—document heavy; regulated
outbound → approval gate essential.

---

### 10. Translation & localization agencies

| Score | 17/25 |

TM glossaries in vault, client style guides, project briefs—RAG + summarize +
QA checklist pipelines. Multilingual EU fit (Greece as hub).

---

### 11. Content / newsletter / creator businesses (B2B creators)

| Score | 16/25 |

Research notes → newsletter drafts → social variants. Constant Contact: 44% use
AI for email/content. BLACKBOX wins on **batch + brand vault + archive**, not
generation quality alone.

---

### 12. Property management (small portfolio)

| Score | 16/25 |

Tenant email triage, maintenance logs, owner monthly reports—Gmail + summarize.
Needs scale of integrations for large PM; micro landlords (5–30 units) fit now.

## Tier B — Possible but harder or slower

| Vertical | Why lower |
|----------|-----------|
| **General retail e-commerce** | Needs Woo/Shopify day one; commodity |
| **Restaurants / hospitality** | Low document; POS-centric |
| **Clinics / HIPAA** | Compliance bar; EHR integration |
| **Law firms** | High liability; malpractice fear; slow sales |
| **Manufacturing floor** | MES/ERP not markdown |
| **Logistics / trucking** | ELD/paperwork but field-mobile first |

## Verticals to deprioritize (honest)

- **Generic “AI for any SMB”** — you lose to ChatGPT
- **Enterprise** — needs SSO, SLA, team you don’t have
- **Fully autonomous outbound** — trust + deliverability kills you
- **Windows desktop RPA** — wrong product shape
- **Crypto / hype verticals** — wrong buyer psychology

---

## Recommended strategy (3 horizons)

**Horizon 1 (now):** Agencies + solo consultants + **your** K-beauty network —
skills that work today.

**Horizon 2 (drivers):** Import/wholesale + recruiting + real estate — Gmail +
search + one commerce API.

**Horizon 3 (packs):** EU grant pack, construction compliance pack, translation
QA pack — YAML libraries, not new kernel.
